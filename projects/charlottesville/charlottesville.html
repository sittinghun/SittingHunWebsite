Charlottesville Woodshop was the final project for W.G. Clark's studio at the University of Virginia Architecture School. The design is a sanctuary in the heart of Charlottesville, made for a couple that crafts and paints wooden toys. It is about protection from the parking garage across the street and surrounding buildings. The house is closed to these elements with frosted glass, but open to its own garden. The ground floor contains the workshop and storefront. The upper two floors are residential, with an open interior.
